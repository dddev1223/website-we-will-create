About the Industry

The automotive industry focuses on the design, production, and sale of motor vehicles. It creates vehicles for people around the world by combining engineering, and business management. In order to make important decisions regarding production, pricing, and sustainability, modern automotive companies rely on technology, and data analytics.

My Role: Financial Analyst

As a Financial Analyst, I review and interpret financial data to help management make informed business decisions. My responsibilities include analyzing production costs, forecasting future expenses, managing budgets, and preparing reports that measure performance across departments. I also work closely with engineering and marketing teams to ensure resources are allocated effectively and profit goals are met.

Two Cybersecurity Issues That Could Arise

  1. Data Breaches: Financial systems store sensitive data and if unauthorized users gain access to those files the company could face financial loss and reputational damage.
  2. Ransomware Attacks: Cybercriminals could encrypt important financial records or production data and demand payment to restore access, halting daily operations and disrupting manufacturing and payroll systems.

Cybersecurity Best Practices

To protect sensitive data and maintain business continuity, the automotive industry should:

  1. Use strong passwords and 2FA to limit unauthorized access.
  2. Regularly back up financial and production data to secure locations.
  3. Keep all financial software and network systems updated with the latest security patches.
  4. Provide cybersecurity training for employees to prevent phishing and social engineering attacks.
