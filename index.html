My Core Values

Values Industry

Trust

  1. Trust is something I learned from my family. Growing up, I was taught that honesty is the start of every strong relationship.
  2. It matters in college because people are meeting me for the first time, and I want them to see me as dependable and real.
  3. When I make a promise, such as finishing my part of a project or showing up prepared, I do my best to keep it.
  4. If I do not stay trustworthy, I risk losing respect and damaging relationships, which makes success harder in school and in life.

Collaboration

  1. I learned the value of collaboration from playing sports and doing group activities where teamwork always gave better results than working alone.
  2. In college, listening to other perspectives helps me grow and see ideas I might not have thought of myself.
  3. Collaboration is important because it makes learning more engaging and prepares me for future careers where teamwork is needed.
  4. If I do not work well with others, I risk missing useful ideas and building a reputation for being difficult to work with.

Excellence

  1. Excellence comes from my desire to make my family proud and to prove to myself that I can reach higher goals.
  2. It matters because it pushes me to put effort into my work instead of turning in something rushed or careless.
  3. Excellence means aiming for steady improvement. I focus on growth rather than perfection.
  4. If I do not strive for excellence, I may fall into poor habits that hold me back both in school and in life.

Accountability

  1. I value accountability because I have seen how much it matters when one person fails to take responsibility. Others often suffer the results.
  2. In college, accountability matters because professors, classmates, and friends are counting on me to meet deadlines and keep my word.
  3. Taking responsibility for mistakes helps me build trust, earn respect, and learn from them.
  4. If I ignore accountability, I risk losing credibility, letting people down, and weakening my own discipline.

Dev Patel, Fall 2025
